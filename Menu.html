Sancocho

Sancocho is a meat and roots-based stew that appears in different forms in several countries of Latin America, especially the countries bordering the Caribbean Sea. It has become one of the most cherished dishes in the Dominican Republic.

Dominican Moro

One of the most common dishes in the Dominican Republic. Moro is a mixture of rice, beans, and vegetables seasoned with herbs and spices for an easy to make, family meal or side dish.

Asopao

Similar to that of a gumbo or jambalaya, Asopao is a chicken and rice pottage that can be served on any day and is guaranteed to make your mouth water making it a Dominican favorite.

Mangu

Mangú is the most popular plantain-based dish in the Dominican Republic, an iconic traditional Dominican breakfast side dish, and one of the most popular Dominican foods. A creamy, smooth plantain mash, Dominican mangu is a great dish to start our days, and an unforgettable treat if you try it for the first time.

Chivo Guisado

Chivo guisado is a Dominican goat stew. It is usually prepared with pieces of goat meat, onions, bitter oranges, garlic, and tomatoes, while the secrets to this delicacy are wild oregano and Scotch bonnet peppers.

Mofongo

Classic plantain mofongo is a dish made of fried plantains, mashed and mixed with garlic paste and chicharrón (crispy pork skin). It is served alongside beef broth. Traditionally, mofongo is served in a pilón, a wooden mortar.
